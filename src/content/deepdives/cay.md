## Problem

Rising competition in the D2C space and dependence on channels like Google and Meta Ads pushed customer acquisition costs higher and higher for independent brands.

## Approach

Build a collaborative network where D2C brands cross-promote each other for mutual growth, through:
- **Brand plugins** — surfacing compatible partner brands on each other's storefronts
- **Data partnerships** — sharing audience insights between brands
- **Collaborations** — bundled offerings and joint social campaigns
- **Rewards** — interconnected loyalty programs

## Space+

Two stakeholders sit on either side of the network: **merchants** and **customers**.

### Merchants

Merchants operate along two axes — **show** (displaying partner brands on their own storefront) and **marketing** (positioning their brand on partner storefronts) — with **matching** deciding who gets paired with whom.

- **Show**: partners appear on the post-checkout order-tracking page; brands control their own customization and which partners they accept.
- **Marketing**: brands choose placement and creative — starting as an uploadable banner with a "check now" button, expandable to fully customizable units — paired with a dedicated coupon code.
- **Matching**: larger brands need control over who they partner with, so matching works two ways — **invite** (bring in an existing contact) or **suggested** (the platform recommends a fit) — with cross-promotion only going live once both sides accept.

### Customer

The customer journey runs **discovery → store → retrieve → apply**: a post-checkout coupon for a partner brand is delivered by email/WhatsApp, sits in the customer's coupon inventory until they're ready, and gets redeemed at the partner's store.

Result: an **11% CTR**, roughly 5x the ~2% CTR typical of other ad formats.

## Ads+

Ad targeting enhanced with brand-level customer behavior data.

**The gap:** ad platforms have little insight into a customer's real spending propensity, actual purchase price range, or willingness to buy from newer D2C brands specifically — marketplace-first shoppers tend to default to established platforms over online-only brands.

**The fix:** connecting brand backend systems surfaces customer personas that transfer to similar audiences via collaborative-filtering algorithms, pushed securely to Meta/Google as custom audiences.

**Results:** a 60% increase in ROAS on Meta and a 20% reduction in CPA.
