As I started my journey with Columbia Business School, one of the first courses we take up is the LEAD.

As part of its examination, which is also the first academic exam many of us have taken after years of working in the corporate, is an interesting one. The course primarily covers various leadership lessons, with scenarios ranging from hiring to negotiations.

The examination has multiple multiple choice questions, and importantly bunch of essays suggesting a plan of action one needs to take as part of a fictional company whose situation has been described as a case.

As I got started on answering the essay question, there was a brief moment of pause. A pause, a writer's block, not because I was struggling with the answer, but the whole aspect of writing.

### AI Usage

Since December 2022, world has witnessed a technical leap, with the public and publicised launch of ChatGPT, AI has become mainstream and a go-to tool for various use cases. One of the key use cases has been to write and improve writing. While I used to churn out blogs of various quality, I was sure about my own writing. It's mine. But with AI, it's easier to evaluate it, improve it and change it. 
What that means, is you can go from thought to full-drawn essay in a flash. It also leads to a lot of sentences and structure being pretty interchangeable.
Long-term one of the issues I found with my own AI usage is the diminishing ability to churn sentences like I used to before. Grammarly, even before AI usage, has impacted my sense of grammar that my english teacher from my high school would be now disappointed at the grammar mistakes I may commit in the first go.
